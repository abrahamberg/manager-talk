# Level 3 Input Questions

For **Level 3**, the coach should ask **experience-based questions**.

The trainee must answer using:

```text
Problem.
Action.
Result.
```

This level moves from simple general answers to **short interview stories**.

***

# Level 3 Goal

The trainee should be able to explain:

1. What the problem was
2. What they did
3. What changed as a result

This is the first level where the trainee should sound **interview-ready**.

***

# Coach Instruction for Level 3

Before each question, the coach should say:

> “Answer with a short story. First say the problem, then what you did, then the result. Keep it to 3–5 sentences.”

The coach should not yet require advanced vocabulary, metrics, or deep leadership theory.

The focus is:

> **clear story, clear action, clear result**

***

# Expected Level 3 Structure

```text
The problem was X.
I did Y.
As a result, Z happened.
```

Or slightly more natural:

```text
The issue was X.
What I did was Y.
The result was Z.
```

***

# Expected Level 3 Answer Pattern

The coach should expect answers like:

```text
The problem was that the team was not aligned on priorities.
I helped clarify the goals and made the work visible in Jira.
As a result, the team understood what mattered most and could focus better.
```

Or:

```text
The issue was that two engineers disagreed about the technical direction.
I listened to both sides and clarified what problem we were actually solving.
As a result, the discussion became more constructive and the team reached a shared decision.
```

***

# Example Level 3 Answers

## Question

> Tell me about a time you improved ways of working.

Good answer:

> “The problem was that the team did not have a shared way of working.  
> I introduced Scrum practices to create more structure and regular communication.  
> As a result, the team became more synchronized and clearer about priorities.”

***

## Question

> Tell me about a time you helped resolve a disagreement.

Good answer:

> “The issue was that two people had different views on the best technical solution.  
> I helped clarify what each person was optimizing for and what outcome we needed.  
> As a result, the team moved from personal disagreement to a shared decision.”

***

## Question

> Tell me about a time you helped someone grow.

Good answer:

> “The problem was that a colleague was unsure about taking ownership of a larger task.  
> I gave them support, clarified the expected outcome, and stayed available for guidance.  
> As a result, they became more confident and completed the work with more independence.”

***

## Question

> Tell me about a time priorities were unclear.

Good answer:

> “The problem was that the team had several requests but no clear priority.  
> I helped compare the work against business goals and clarified what should come first.  
> As a result, the team focused on the most important work instead of spreading effort too thin.”

***

## Question

> Tell me about a time you led without formal authority.

Good answer:

> “The issue was that the team needed direction, but there was no clear owner for the coordination.  
> I stepped in to organize the work, clarify responsibilities, and follow up on progress.  
> As a result, the team moved forward with more structure and less confusion.”

***

# Level 3 Pass Criteria

The trainee passes one answer if:

* It is **3–5 sentences**
* It clearly states the **problem**
* It clearly states **what the trainee did**
* It clearly states the **result**
* It does not include too much background
* It does not drift into unrelated details
* The trainee’s personal contribution is obvious

***

# Level 3 Scoring

The coach can score each answer out of 8.

## 1. Problem

* 0 = no problem stated
* 1 = problem is vague
* 2 = problem is clear

## 2. Action

* 0 = no action stated
* 1 = action is vague
* 2 = action is clear

## 3. Result

* 0 = no result stated
* 1 = result is weak or unclear
* 2 = result is clear

## 4. Conciseness

* 0 = rambling
* 1 = somewhat concise
* 2 = concise

## Passing Score

The trainee passes the answer with:

```text
7 or 8 out of 8
```

***

# Common Level 3 Mistakes

## Mistake 1 — Too much background

Weak answer:

> “When I joined the team, there were many things going on and people had different opinions and we were trying to work in an agile way but it was not really working and then after some time I started to help…”

Why it fails:

* Too long before the point
* No clear problem
* Listener gets lost

Better:

> “The problem was that the team had no shared way of working.  
> I introduced more structure through planning and regular communication.  
> As a result, the team became more aligned.”

***

## Mistake 2 — Vague action

Weak answer:

> “I helped the team communicate better.”

Coach asks:

> “What did you actually do?”

Better:

> “I introduced regular planning and made work visible in Jira.”

***

## Mistake 3 — Weak result

Weak answer:

> “It was better after that.”

Coach asks:

> “What specifically became better?”

Better:

> “The team became more synchronized and had fewer misunderstandings about priorities.”

***

## Mistake 4 — No personal contribution

Weak answer:

> “The team improved the process and then things got better.”

Coach asks:

> “What did you do?”

Better:

> “I took the Scrum Master role and introduced a clearer working rhythm.”

***

## Mistake 5 — Too abstract

Weak answer:

> “I created alignment and ownership.”

Coach asks:

> “How?”

Better:

> “I clarified the goal, made responsibilities visible, and followed up on progress.”

***

# Coach Correction Script

If the trainee gives a weak Level 3 answer, the coach should say:

```text
Good idea. Now make it Level 3.

Sentence 1: What was the problem?
Sentence 2: What did you do?
Sentence 3: What was the result?
```

If the answer is still unclear, ask:

```text
What was broken or unclear?
What action did you personally take?
What changed after your action?
```

Then ask the trainee to repeat the full answer.

***

# Level 3 Completion Standard

The trainee is ready to move to Level 4 when they can answer **10 different Level 3 questions** and pass at least **8 out of 10** without coach support.

They should be able to consistently answer in this form:

```text
The problem was X.
I did Y.
As a result, Z happened.
```

At this level, the trainee does **not** need advanced vocabulary yet.

They only need to be:

> **clear, structured, and easy to follow.**
