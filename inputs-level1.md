Yes. For **Level 1**, the coach should ask **very simple questions** where the trainee only needs to answer:

```text

```

The goal is **not storytelling yet**.  
The goal is only: **action + result**.

# Coach Instruction for Level 1

For every question, the coach should say:

> “Answer in two sentences only. First sentence: what you did. Second sentence: what happened as a result.”

The trainee should use this pattern:

```text
I [action].
As a result, [result].
```

***

# Example Level 1 Answers

## Question

> How do you handle many tasks?

Good answer:

> I prioritize the most important tasks first.  
> As a result, I stay focused and finish the most valuable work.

***

## Question

> How do you help someone who is blocked?

Good answer:

> I ask what is blocking them and help remove the obstacle.  
> As a result, they can continue their work.

***

## Question

> How do you keep your team informed?

Good answer:

> I share clear updates about progress and blockers.  
> As a result, the team knows what is happening and can plan better.

***

# Level 1 Pass Criteria

The trainee passes one answer if:

* It has **maximum 2 sentences**
* Sentence 1 says **what I did**
* Sentence 2 says **the result**
* No long explanation
* No extra story
* No unrelated details

***

# Coach Correction

If the trainee rambles, the coach should interrupt gently and say:

> “Good idea. Now say it again in only two sentences: what you did and what happened.”

If the result is missing, ask:

> “What changed because of your action?”

If the action is unclear, ask:

> “What did you actually do?”

***

# Level 1 Practice Rule

The trainee should not move to Level 2 until they can answer **10 different Level 1 questions clearly** using:

```text
I did X.
As a result, Y happened.
```
