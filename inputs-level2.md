# Level 2 Input Questions

For **Level 2**, the coach should ask questions where the trainee must answer with:

```text
Point.
Action.
Result.
```

The goal is to train the trainee to **start with the main idea first**, then explain what they do, then explain why it helps.

***

# Level 2 Goal

The trainee should be able to answer in **3 short sentences**:

```text
The main point is X.
I do Y.
This helps with Z.
```

Or more naturally:

```text
X is important.
I do Y.
This helps Z happen.
```

***

# Coach Instruction for Level 2

Before each question, the coach should say:

> “Answer in three sentences. First sentence: your main point. Second sentence: what you do. Third sentence: why it helps.”

The coach should not allow long stories yet.

This level is still about **basic structure**, not advanced examples.

# Expected Level 2 Answer Pattern

The coach should expect answers like this:

```text
Clear communication is important for alignment.
I make sure work and priorities are visible to everyone.
This helps the team understand what matters and work in the same direction.
```

Or:

```text
Prioritization is important when there are too many tasks.
I focus first on the work that has the highest value or urgency.
This helps me stay focused and deliver the most important outcomes.
```

***

# Example Level 2 Answers

## Question

> How do you make sure your team is aligned?

Good answer:

> Communication is key for alignment.  
> I make sure priorities and work are visible, for example in Jira.  
> This helps everyone understand what matters and work in the same direction.

***

## Question

> How do you handle disagreement?

Good answer:

> Understanding the root cause is important in disagreement.  
> I listen to both sides and clarify what the disagreement is really about.  
> This helps the team resolve the issue without becoming defensive.

***

## Question

> How do you create ownership?

Good answer:

> Clarity is important for ownership.  
> I make sure people understand the goal, the expected outcome, and their responsibility.  
> This helps them take ownership instead of waiting for detailed instructions.

***

## Question

> How do you communicate risk?

Good answer:

> Early communication is important when there is risk.  
> I explain what the risk is, what impact it may have, and what options we have.  
> This helps the team and stakeholders make better decisions early.

***

## Question

> How do you help someone grow?

Good answer:

> Growth starts with understanding the person’s strengths and development areas.  
> I listen, give feedback, and delegate work that helps them stretch.  
> This helps them build confidence and improve over time.

***

# Level 2 Pass Criteria

The trainee passes one answer if:

* It has **3 sentences maximum**
* Sentence 1 gives the **main point**
* Sentence 2 explains **what they do**
* Sentence 3 explains **why it helps**
* The answer is clear without extra story
* The trainee does not ramble
* The trainee does not only use abstract words without explanation

***

# Level 2 Scoring

The coach can score each answer out of 6.

## 1. Main Point

* 0 = no main point
* 1 = weak or unclear main point
* 2 = clear main point

## 2. Action

* 0 = no action
* 1 = vague action
* 2 = clear action

## 3. Result

* 0 = no result
* 1 = weak result
* 2 = clear result

## Passing Score

The trainee passes the answer with:

```text
5 or 6 out of 6
```

***

# Common Level 2 Mistakes

## Mistake 1 — Starting with explanation instead of point

Weak answer:

> “Usually there are many people and different priorities, so I try to talk to them and understand what is happening…”

Coach correction:

> “Start with the main point first.”

Better:

> “Alignment starts with clear priorities.  
> I make sure priorities are visible and discussed.  
> This helps the team work in the same direction.”

***

## Mistake 2 — Action is too vague

Weak answer:

> “Communication is important.  
> I communicate with people.  
> This helps the team.”

Coach correction:

> “What do you actually communicate?”

Better:

> “Communication is important for alignment.  
> I share priorities, progress, and blockers clearly.  
> This helps the team understand what is happening and what matters.”

***

## Mistake 3 — No result

Weak answer:

> “Prioritization is important.  
> I check what is important and work on that.”

Coach correction:

> “Add why it helps.”

Better:

> “Prioritization is important when there are too many tasks.  
> I focus first on the work that matters most.  
> This helps me stay focused and deliver the highest-value work.”

***

## Mistake 4 — Too many ideas

Weak answer:

> “Accountability is important, and communication is important, and people should own things, and also you need to follow up and build trust.”

Coach correction:

> “Choose one main point.”

Better:

> “Clarity is important for accountability.  
> I make sure people understand the goal and who owns what.  
> This helps the team follow through on commitments.”

***

# Coach Correction Script

If the trainee gives a weak answer, the coach should use this script:

```text
Good idea. Now make it Level 2.

Sentence 1: What is the main point?
Sentence 2: What do you do?
Sentence 3: Why does it help?
```

If the trainee still struggles, the coach should ask:

```text
What is the one main idea?
What action do you take?
What changes because of that action?
```

Then ask the trainee to repeat the full answer in 3 sentences.

***

# Level 2 Completion Standard

The trainee is ready to move to Level 3 when they can answer **10 different Level 2 questions** with:

```text
Point.
Action.
Result.
```

and pass at least **8 out of 10** answers without coach support.
