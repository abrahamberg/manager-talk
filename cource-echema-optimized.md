# Optimized Communication Ladder For LLM Context

Use this compact ladder to evaluate the user's answer at the current level only. Do not require higher-level skills before the user reaches that level.

## Global Coaching Rules

- Structure comes before advanced vocabulary.
- Feedback must be concise and practical.
- Good answers match the current level structure, answer the question directly, and stop cleanly.
- Treat speech-to-text grammar, spelling, punctuation, and missing sentence boundaries generously when the meaning is clear.
- Do not fail an answer only because it needs wording cleanup.
- Weak answers usually miss structure, result, concrete change, judgment, or strategic connection depending on level.
- Next question should be chosen by coaching reason from the most useful category in the same level, not by file order.

## Level 1: Action + Result

- Expected: `I did X. As a result, Y happened.`
- Pass: maximum 2 sentences, one clear action, one clear result, no extra story.
- Also pass if action and result are both semantically present in one run-on sentence caused by speech-to-text.
- If action and result are clear but wording has grammar mistakes, score 4 and give a cleaner version.
- Coach focus: make the user finish a clear thought.

## Level 2: Point + Action + Result

- Expected: `The main point is X. I do Y. This helps with Z.`
- Pass: starts with main point, explains action and why it matters in 3 sentences or fewer.
- Coach focus: lead with the point before explaining.

## Level 3: Problem + Action + Result

- Expected: `The problem was X. I did Y. The result was Z.`
- Pass: clear short story, user's own action is obvious, outcome is clear, no rambling background.
- Coach focus: interview-ready story structure.

## Level 4: Situation + Action + Visible Change

- Expected: `The situation was X. The issue was Y. I did Z. The visible change was A. The result was B.`
- Pass: concrete example, believable before/after, visible behavior change, not only abstract claims.
- Coach focus: make impact concrete.

## Level 5: Principle + Practice + Example + Outcome

- Expected: `My principle is X. In practice, I do Y. For example, Z. The outcome is A.`
- Pass: states leadership belief, applies it practically, gives example, explains outcome.
- Coach focus: sound intentional as a manager.

## Level 6: Balance Leadership Tensions

- Expected: `There are two sides. One side is X. The other side is Y. I balance them by doing Z. The result is A.`
- Pass: identifies both sides, avoids simplistic answer, explains balancing behavior and result.
- Coach focus: leadership judgment under tension.

## Level 7: Diagnose + Adapt + Optimize

- Expected: `First, I diagnose context. I look at X, Y, Z. Then I adapt. If A, I do B. If C, I do D. The goal is E.`
- Pass: diagnoses before acting, adapts to team maturity/context, avoids rigid process thinking.
- Coach focus: senior manager adaptability.

## Level 8: Connect Team Work To Business Outcomes

- Expected: `The business need was X. The team challenge was Y. I helped the team do Z. This improved A. The business impact was B.`
- Pass: explains business context, team outcomes, priorities/tradeoffs, and value creation.
- Coach focus: outcome over output.

## Level 9: Executive Presence

- Expected: `My view is X. The reason is Y. In practice, I do Z. The impact is A. The principle is B.`
- Pass: clear, calm, decisive, concise, confident, strong ending, connects people/technology/business.
- Coach focus: high-signal leadership communication.

## Level 10: System Leadership + Strategic Judgment

- Expected: `The broader context is X. The system challenge is Y. My approach is Z. I create alignment through A, accountability through B, sustainability through C. The outcome is D.`
- Pass: talks about teams as systems, connects people/process/technology/business, shows sustainability and judgment.
- Coach focus: scalable senior manager communication.

## Level 11: Organization-Level Thinking

- Expected: `At the organizational level, the challenge is X. The risk is Y. The leadership priority is Z. I would address it through A, B, C. Success measure is D.`
- Pass: talks beyond one team, identifies org bottlenecks, balances standardization/autonomy, defines success measures.
- Coach focus: director-level clarity across teams.

## Vocabulary Guidance

- Levels 1-2: simple words only; clarity matters most.
- Levels 3-5: alignment, ownership, clarity, priorities, accountability, outcome.
- Levels 6-8: psychological safety, autonomy, stakeholder alignment, tradeoffs, operating model, outcome over output.
- Levels 9-10: strategic context, system thinking, sustainable delivery, leadership leverage, team health, business impact.
- Level 11: organizational effectiveness, dependency management, decision latency, governance, operating cadence, capability building.
