# Level 4 Input Questions

For **Level 4**, the coach should ask questions where the trainee must give a **real example** and describe a **visible change**.

This level moves from:

> “I did something and it helped”

to:

> “Here is what was happening, here is what I changed, and here is what became visibly different.”

***

# Level 4 Goal

The trainee should be able to answer with:

1. Situation
2. Issue
3. Action
4. Visible change
5. Result

The focus is **concrete impact**.

At this level, the trainee should not only say:

> “The team improved.”

They should say:

> “The team started doing X differently.”

***

# Coach Instruction for Level 4

Before each question, the coach should say:

> “Answer with a concrete example. Describe the situation, what was not working, what you did, what changed visibly, and the result. Keep it to 4–6 sentences.”

The coach should push for **observable behavior**, not abstract words.

***

# Expected Level 4 Structure

```text
The situation was X.
The issue was Y.
I did Z.
The visible change was A.
The result was B.
```

Or more naturally:

```text
When I joined/started/worked on X, the situation was Y.
The main issue was Z.
I changed A by doing B.
After that, we could see C.
As a result, D improved.
```

# Expected Level 4 Answer Pattern

The coach should expect answers like:

```text
When I joined the team, the ways of working were unclear.
The main issue was that people had different expectations about priorities and ownership.
I introduced a clearer planning rhythm and made work visible in Jira.
The visible change was that people started discussing the same priorities and blockers earlier.
As a result, the team became more synchronized and easier to coordinate.
```

Or:

```text
The situation was that two engineers disagreed about a technical direction.
The issue was not only the solution, but that they were optimizing for different things.
I helped clarify the assumptions behind each option and brought the discussion back to the outcome we wanted.
The visible change was that the discussion became less personal and more focused on tradeoffs.
As a result, the team reached a shared decision faster.
```

# Example Level 4 Answers

## Question

> Tell me about a time you improved the team’s ways of working.

Good answer:

> “When I joined the team, our ways of working were not clear.  
> The main issue was that people were not synchronized around priorities, planning, and ownership.  
> I took on the Scrum Master role and introduced clearer ceremonies, better Jira visibility, and a shared planning rhythm.  
> The visible change was that the team started discussing priorities and blockers earlier instead of discovering them late.  
> As a result, the team became more aligned and eventually mature enough to simplify the process.”

***

## Question

> Tell me about a time you improved communication.

Good answer:

> “The situation was that updates were happening in different places, so people had different understandings of progress.  
> The issue was lack of visibility.  
> I made sure progress, blockers, and priorities were documented in one place and discussed regularly.  
> The visible change was that fewer people asked for repeated updates because the information was easier to find.  
> As a result, communication became smoother and the team could focus more on delivery.”

***

## Question

> Tell me about a time you helped someone take ownership.

Good answer:

> “A colleague was hesitant to take ownership of a larger task because the expected outcome was unclear.  
> The issue was not ability, but confidence and clarity.  
> I helped define the expected result, broke the work into smaller steps, and stayed available for support.  
> The visible change was that the colleague started making decisions independently and asking more focused questions.  
> As a result, they completed the work with more confidence and less dependency on others.”

***

## Question

> Tell me about a time you resolved disagreement.

Good answer:

> “Two people had different opinions about the best technical solution.  
> The issue was that they were using different language and optimizing for different risks.  
> I listened to both sides and clarified what each person was really concerned about.  
> The visible change was that they realized they were aiming for a similar outcome.  
> As a result, the discussion became constructive and the team could agree on a direction.”

***

## Question

> Tell me about a time you improved accountability.

Good answer:

> “The situation was that tasks were moving slowly because ownership was unclear.  
> The issue was that everyone agreed something was important, but no one clearly owned the next step.  
> I helped clarify responsibilities and made ownership visible in our planning.  
> The visible change was that people started following up on their own areas without waiting to be asked.  
> As a result, execution became more predictable.”

***

# Level 4 Pass Criteria

The trainee passes one answer if:

* It is **4–6 sentences**
* It gives a **real or realistic concrete example**
* It states the **situation**
* It states the **issue**
* It explains **what the trainee did**
* It describes a **visible change**
* It explains the **result**
* It does not become a long story
* It avoids only abstract language

***

# Level 4 Scoring

The coach can score each answer out of 10.

## 1. Situation

* 0 = no situation
* 1 = vague situation
* 2 = clear situation

## 2. Issue

* 0 = no issue
* 1 = vague issue
* 2 = clear issue

## 3. Action

* 0 = no action
* 1 = vague action
* 2 = clear action

## 4. Visible Change

* 0 = no visible change
* 1 = weak or generic change
* 2 = clear visible change

## 5. Result

* 0 = no result
* 1 = weak result
* 2 = clear result

## Passing Score

The trainee passes the answer with:

```text
8 out of 10 or higher
```

# Common Level 4 Mistakes

## Mistake 1 — No visible change

Weak answer:

> “I improved communication and the team became better.”

Why it fails:

* “Better” is too vague
* No observable change

Coach asks:

> “What would I have seen differently in the team?”

Better:

> “People started raising blockers earlier and checking priorities before starting new work.”

***

## Mistake 2 — Too abstract

Weak answer:

> “I created alignment and ownership.”

Coach asks:

> “What did you actually change?”

Better:

> “I made priorities visible in Jira, clarified owners, and added regular planning discussions.”

***

## Mistake 3 — Too much background

Weak answer:

> “When I joined, there were many different things happening, and the team had some history, and people were trying to work in agile but not really, and there were many reasons…”

Coach correction:

> “Start with the situation in one sentence.”

Better:

> “When I joined, the team did not have a shared way of working.”

***

## Mistake 4 — Result is not connected to action

Weak answer:

> “I introduced Jira improvements. As a result, the business was happier.”

Coach asks:

> “How did your action create that result?”

Better:

> “I introduced Jira improvements so priorities and blockers were visible. As a result, stakeholders had clearer status and fewer surprises.”

***

## Mistake 5 — Personal contribution is unclear

Weak answer:

> “The process improved over time and the team became more mature.”

Coach asks:

> “What did you personally do?”

Better:

> “I took the Scrum Master role, introduced a planning rhythm, and helped the team reflect on what ceremonies were useful.”

***

# Coach Correction Script

If the trainee gives a weak Level 4 answer, the coach should say:

```text
Good idea. Now make it Level 4.

Sentence 1: What was the situation?
Sentence 2: What was the issue?
Sentence 3: What did you do?
Sentence 4: What visibly changed?
Sentence 5: What was the result?
```

If the trainee struggles, ask:

```text
What was unclear or broken?
What did you personally change?
What would someone observe after your change?
What improved because of that?
```

Then ask the trainee to repeat the answer.

***

# Coach Push Questions for Level 4

Use these when the answer is too vague:

```text
What did you actually do?
What changed in the team’s behavior?
What became easier after that?
What became more visible?
What happened less often?
What happened more often?
How would a stakeholder notice the improvement?
How would a teammate notice the improvement?
What was different before and after?
```

***

# Level 4 Completion Standard

The trainee is ready to move to Level 5 when they can answer **10 different Level 4 questions** and pass at least **8 out of 10** without coach support.

They should consistently be able to describe:

```text
Situation.
Issue.
Action.
Visible change.
Result.
```

At this level, the trainee should sound:

> **concrete, believable, and easy to follow.**
