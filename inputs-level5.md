# Level 5 Input Questions

For **Level 5**, the coach should ask questions where the trainee must explain a **leadership principle**, how they apply it in practice, and what outcome it creates.

This level moves from:

> “Here is what I did”

to:

> “Here is the leadership principle behind what I did, how I apply it, and why it works.”

***

# Level 5 Goal

The trainee should be able to answer with:

1. **Principle** — what they believe as a leader
2. **Practice** — what they actually do
3. **Example** — how it shows up in real work
4. **Outcome** — what it improves

At this level, the trainee starts sounding like a **manager**, not only a team member or tech lead.

***

# Coach Instruction for Level 5

Before each question, the coach should say:

> “Answer with a leadership principle first. Then explain how you apply it in practice, give a short example, and explain the outcome. Keep it to 4–6 sentences.”

The coach should push the trainee to avoid only describing tasks.

The trainee should not only say:

> “I clarify ownership.”

They should say:

> “My principle is that accountability starts with clarity. In practice, I clarify ownership, expected outcomes, and follow-up.”

***

# Expected Level 5 Structure

```text
My principle is X.
In practice, I do Y.
For example, Z.
The outcome is A.
```

Or more naturally:

```text
I believe X is important because Y.
In practice, I do Z.
For example, when A happens, I do B.
This creates C.
```

# Expected Level 5 Answer Pattern

The coach should expect answers like:

```text
My principle is that accountability starts with clarity.
In practice, I make sure the goal, expected outcome, and owner are clear.
For example, when work is unclear, I help define what success looks like and who owns the next step.
This creates transparency and makes follow-through easier without micromanagement.
```

Or:

```text
I believe delegation should create both delivery and growth.
In practice, I delegate ownership, not only tasks.
For example, I give someone a clear outcome and enough context, then stay available for support.
This helps people grow while still keeping the work aligned.
```

***

# Example Level 5 Answers

## Question

> What is your approach to creating accountability?

Good answer:

> “My principle is that accountability starts with clarity.  
> In practice, I make sure people understand the goal, the expected outcome, and who owns what.  
> For example, when a task is unclear, I clarify the next step and the decision owner.  
> This creates transparency and helps people follow through without needing micromanagement.”

***

## Question

> How do you delegate without micromanaging?

Good answer:

> “My principle is to delegate outcomes, not just tasks.  
> In practice, I explain the goal, constraints, and expected result, then give the person space to decide how to solve it.  
> For example, instead of telling someone every step, I agree on the desired outcome and check in at useful points.  
> This gives autonomy while still keeping alignment.”

***

## Question

> How do you help someone grow?

Good answer:

> “I believe growth happens when people get the right level of challenge and support.  
> In practice, I look at the person’s strengths, development areas, and current confidence level.  
> For example, I may delegate a slightly bigger responsibility and stay available for guidance.  
> This helps the person stretch without feeling abandoned.”

***

## Question

> How do you create alignment?

Good answer:

> “My principle is that alignment comes from shared context, not only status updates.  
> In practice, I make sure the team understands the business goal, priorities, and expected outcomes.  
> For example, I connect Jira work to the broader goal so people understand why the work matters.  
> This helps the team make better decisions independently.”

***

## Question

> How do you give feedback?

Good answer:

> “My principle is that feedback should be clear, respectful, and actionable.  
> In practice, I focus on specific behavior and its impact, not on the person’s character.  
> For example, instead of saying someone communicates badly, I explain where information was missing and what would help next time.  
> This makes feedback easier to accept and easier to act on.”

***

# Level 5 Pass Criteria

The trainee passes one answer if:

* It is **4–6 sentences**
* It starts with a **clear leadership principle**
* It explains the **practical behavior**
* It includes a **short example**
* It explains the **outcome**
* It sounds intentional, not accidental
* It avoids vague management words without explanation
* It does not become too long or theoretical

***

# Level 5 Scoring

The coach can score each answer out of 10.

## 1. Leadership Principle

* 0 = no principle
* 1 = vague principle
* 2 = clear principle

## 2. Practice

* 0 = no practical action
* 1 = vague action
* 2 = clear practical action

## 3. Example

* 0 = no example
* 1 = weak or generic example
* 2 = clear example

## 4. Outcome

* 0 = no outcome
* 1 = weak outcome
* 2 = clear outcome

## 5. Manager Maturity

* 0 = sounds like task execution only
* 1 = somewhat managerial
* 2 = sounds like intentional leadership

## Passing Score

The trainee passes the answer with:

```text
8 out of 10 or higher
```

***

# Common Level 5 Mistakes

## Mistake 1 — Only saying what you did

Weak answer:

> “I clarify ownership and follow up.”

Why it fails:

* No leadership principle
* Sounds tactical only

Better:

> “My principle is that accountability starts with clarity. I clarify ownership and follow up so people know what is expected.”

***

## Mistake 2 — Too theoretical

Weak answer:

> “Leadership is about empowerment, trust, accountability, transparency, ownership, and psychological safety.”

Why it fails:

* Too many abstract words
* No practical action
* No example

Better:

> “My principle is that empowerment requires clear context. In practice, I explain the goal and constraints, then give people space to solve the problem.”

***

## Mistake 3 — No example

Weak answer:

> “I help people grow by understanding their strengths and giving them opportunities.”

Coach asks:

> “Give a short example of what that looks like.”

Better:

> “For example, if someone is strong technically but less confident leading discussions, I ask them to lead a technical review while I support them before and after.”

***

## Mistake 4 — Outcome is too vague

Weak answer:

> “This helps the team.”

Coach asks:

> “Helps how?”

Better:

> “This helps the team make decisions faster and take ownership without waiting for detailed instructions.”

***

## Mistake 5 — Principle does not match action

Weak answer:

> “My principle is autonomy. I tell people exactly what to do.”

Why it fails:

* Contradiction between principle and practice

Better:

> “My principle is autonomy with alignment. I explain the expected outcome and constraints, then let people choose the implementation approach.”

***

# Coach Correction Script

If the trainee gives a weak Level 5 answer, the coach should say:

```text
Good idea. Now make it Level 5.

Sentence 1: What is your leadership principle?
Sentence 2: What do you do in practice?
Sentence 3: Give a short example.
Sentence 4: What outcome does this create?
```

If the trainee struggles, ask:

```text
What do you believe as a leader?
How do you apply that belief?
What does it look like in real work?
What improves because of it?
```

Then ask the trainee to repeat the full answer.

***

# Coach Push Questions for Level 5

Use these when the answer is too vague:

```text
What is the principle behind that?
What do you do differently because of that principle?
What does that look like in practice?
Can you give a small example?
What outcome does that create?
How does this help the team?
How does this reduce micromanagement?
How does this create ownership?
How does this support business goals?
```

***

# Level 5 Completion Standard

The trainee is ready to move to Level 6 when they can answer **10 different Level 5 questions** and pass at least **8 out of 10** without coach support.

They should consistently be able to answer in this form:

```text
My principle is X.
In practice, I do Y.
For example, Z.
The outcome is A.
```

At this level, the trainee should sound:

> **intentional, practical, and manager-like.**
